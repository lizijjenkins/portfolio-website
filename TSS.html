The Science Spot

The Science Spot provides educators one spot to access the resources, community, and expert advise they need to be an outstanding educator.

My Role
User Reseach, IA/UX/UI Desing, Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing
Tools
Miro, Helio, Figma, Canva, Balsamiq, Typeform, Optimal Workshop

Case Foundation

Science educators need a way to find premade lessons and teaching guided for specific standards because they wish to give thier studnets the best education possible

Design Process

I used the UX Design Process as a guide while completing this project. I started the discovery phase where my focus was research and getting to know my problem area. This lead me to the concepting phase where I determined features and brainstormed solutions. Lastly I refined my project with Prototyping and testing.

My Discovery

To kickstart the Discovery phase, I began by analyzing competitors and conducting SWOT and UX analyses. During this stage, I pinpointed several promising opportunities. Following this, I concentrated on readying myself for user interviews by establishing precise research objectives and identifying my target audience. Following the interviews, I used affinity mapping to structure my discoveries. This method facilitated the acquisition of valuable insights to be applied in the subsequent stages of the project.

My concepting

During this phase of the design process, the primary emphasis is placed on user-centric considerations. Building upon the insights derived from the discovery phase, I meticulously craft two distinct user personas. Subsequently, these personas serve as the foundation for the formulation of user journeys, enabling a granular understanding of the sequential steps users would undertake to accomplish specific tasks. These insights are further channeled into the creation of user flows, effectively delineating the expected interaction pathways. To orchestrate the systematic organization of the application's features, intended for utilization by the identified personas, I develop a comprehensive site map. I then conducted card sorting in order to refine my sitemap.

Card Sorting

A closed card sorting test was conducted via Optimal Workshop and 6 users participated. The card sort revealed the need to minimize the grouping and reorganize a couple of pages/screens. The areas that had mixed results confirmed the assumption that some content will be duplicated on several pages. The new sitemap includes updated labels and hierarchy.

Usability Test Plan

The goal of this usability test is to find out if the design is truly intuitive. I would like to observe and measure if users understand the app, its value, and how to complete basic initial functions such as Signing Up, Searching for a resource, uploading a resource and chatting with other app users. I will be conducting six in person moderated test in a local coffee shop. The participants ranged in age, and tecnology experience but they all had education experience.

Usability Test Results

Participants found the app very useful and easy to use. They didn’t make any critical errors, however, improved button placement and interface design would make task completion more time effective and smooth.
